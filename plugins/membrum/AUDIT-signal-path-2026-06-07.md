## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — Root causes of "presets sound the same and boring"

The sameness is driven by **gain-staging collapse at the voice output**, compounded by **the timbre-carrying modal body being buried under broadband layers**, and by **several headline timbral parameters being inert on most body types**. In order of contribution:

1. **The per-voice `softClip` runs as the de-facto gain stage, not a safety net.** Verified by running the cited tests: Kick and all toms peak at *exactly* 0.5012 (−6 dBFS) at *both* velocity 1.0 and 0.5 — peak velocity response is zero. `diagnose_orch` at `level=0.10` still shows peak 1.0 with 7588/24000 samples ≥0.99 (near-square). The chain reaches the clipper ~30× too hot, so the clip nonlinearity — identical for every voice — pins the loudest pads to one ceiling and imposes one timbral signature. (`drum_voice.h:446,769,782,885`)

2. **The modal body is ~17 dB too quiet relative to the noise (×3) and click (×2) layers.** The body is scaled by `1/sqrt(N)` (0.144 for 48-mode Membrane) with the documented make-up gain (`bodyGainCompensation_`) dead-coded to 1.0. Meanwhile the broadband noise/click layers are summed un-attenuated at high default mixes (noise 0.5–0.95, click 0.4–0.75). The ear hears the noisy thwack; Material/Size/StrikePos/airLoading/damping — the *only* stage that carries them — is masked. (`drum_voice.h:248,251,433-435,743-748`)

3. **An entire family of motion parameters is inert on 5 of 6 bodies.** The **pitch envelope** (Start/End/Time/Curve/Knee/Mid) and **Material Morph** only re-configure the bank for `Membrane`; for Plate/Shell/Bell/String/NoiseBody the per-sample/per-block refresh early-returns. An 808-style pitch drop on a Plate or Bell does nothing — a primary axis of variation collapses to static hits. (`drum_voice.h:1158-1200,1190-1200,486-487,603-621`)

4. **The per-pad Level knob cannot escape the clipper.** Because `level_` multiplies *inside* `softClip(shaped*env*level)`, an 18 dB cut (0.8→0.1) produces almost no attenuation once the voice is saturated — the user-reported "distortion that doesn't respond to preset-level cuts." (`drum_voice.h:446,769,782`)

5. **Physics-side: the Plate/cymbal body uses a too-ordered SSSS `(m²+n²)/2` ratio ladder** (with literal degenerate repeats) instead of a free-plate / Chladni `(m+2n)^P` inharmonic spectrum, and the **Stretch (inharmonicity) knob is under-applied** (off-by-one index + B capped at 0.001). Metallic presets converge on the same skeletal spectrum because the inharmonic fingerprint that distinguishes one cymbal/gong from another is not in the parameter space.

**Critical sequencing note:** fixes #1, #2, #4 are gain-staging; they MUST land before presets are re-tuned, because the current presets were voiced against a clipped/buried chain and re-tuning against the broken model wastes the effort.

## 2. CODE FINDINGS BY SEVERITY

### HIGH

**H-1. Per-voice `softClip` runs in full saturation as the primary gain stage** — `drum_voice.h:446,769,782,885`
- Bug: every voice ends with `softClip(shaped*env*level_)`; the chain arrives ~30× over the clip threshold. Measured (test-run): Kick + all toms pin to 0.5012 at vel 1.0 AND 0.5; `diagnose_orch` level=0.10 → peak 1.0, 7588 samples ≥0.99.
- Fix: keep the per-voice output **linear** and bound the *summed* bus downstream (master limiter / true-peak brickwall). If saturation is wanted, make it an explicit input-gain-driven drive control, not a hidden always-on tanh at the hottest node (per STK Modal / Faust physmodels / Bilbao — none apply a tanh at the bank/voice output).
- Audible win: restores per-velocity peak dynamics and lets distinct bodies sound distinct instead of one saturated square.

**H-2. Modal body ~17 dB under un-attenuated noise(×3)/click(×2) layers** — `drum_voice.h:248,251,433-435,743-748`; `noise_layer.h:218`; `click_layer.h:153`
- Bug: body `= 1/sqrt(48)=0.144` with `bodyGainCompensation_` hard-set to 1.0 (dead make-up). Noise/click summed at full standalone gain; default kit mixes are high. The layers (filtered noise + raised-cosine click) don't change with body knobs, so they dominate and mask the body's Material/Size/StrikePos/damping character. (Note: the noise constant was calibrated against the *pre-Phase-11* un-attenuated body, so the imbalance is a calibration regression.)
- Fix: re-balance — either restore amplitude-aware body make-up (use the bank's existing `getInputGainSum()` for a `1/Σ|aᵢ|` unit-peak ceiling) or lower the noise/click standalone gains to match the post-`1/sqrt(N)` body level, then re-derive default mixes.
- Audible win: body timbre re-emerges; Material/Size/airLoading/damping become audible again.

**H-3. Pitch envelope inert for Plate/Shell/String/Bell/NoiseBody** — `drum_voice.h:1190-1200,486-487,603-621`
- Bug: `updateBodyFundamental()` only does work for Membrane; block paths gate on `pitchEnvForMembrane`. Worse than reported: the mappers also ignore `pitchHz` (`/*pitchHz*/`), so for 5/6 bodies *not even the start frequency* is applied — the whole Start/End/Time/Curve/Knee/Mid family is fully dead.
- Fix: extend `updateBodyFundamental()` to rescale each body's bank f0 for all body types (and have the mappers consume `pitchHz`), or at minimum apply the envelope as a multiplicative f0 scale uniformly across bodies.
- Audible win: 808-style sweeps and per-hit pitch motion work on every body — a primary expressive axis restored.

**H-4. Per-pad Level applied *inside* softClip — cannot attenuate an over-driven voice** — `drum_voice.h:446,769,782`
- Bug: `softClip(shaped*env*level_)`; reducing `level_` slides along the clipper's flat region. `diagnose_orch` level=0.10 still clips.
- Fix: move Level (and the master makeup) *after* the saturation/limiter once H-1 is done: `softClip(shaped*env) * level` (or fully linear voice → post-limiter level).
- Audible win: Level becomes a real headroom control; presets differing only in level/velocity stop sounding identical.

> Note: H-1 and H-4 are the same line; fixing the gain staging (linear voice + downstream limiter + post-clip level) resolves both. Verdicts split them because the *mechanisms* (dynamics crush vs. dead level control) are distinct.

### MEDIUM

**M-1. Material Morph sweep dropped for non-Membrane bodies** — `drum_voice.h:1158-1200,405-409,629-643`
- Bug: morph refresh re-runs the mapper only for Membrane; for other bodies the morph counter advances but the mapper is never re-applied. Static start value reaches the body; the intra-hit sweep is lost. (Opt-in / off by default in factory kit, hence medium not high.)
- Fix: same as H-3 — re-run the body mapper on morph change for all body types.

**M-2. Drive stage applies up to ~9× un-compensated makeup, compressing dynamics** — `tone_shaper.h:198-203,387-394`
- Bug: drive maps to internal gain 1–10 into `recipSqrt` with no makeup; small-signal boosted ~9× while peaks pin to ±1 → hard compressor/limiter. Only bites at high drive (bypassed at 0).
- Fix: add post-shaper makeup normalization (≈1/g in the unity region) or reduce the drive range; treat as flavor saturator, not a compressor.

**M-3. NonlinearCoupling applies recipSqrt to the ENTIRE signal whenever amount≠0** — `nonlinear_coupling.h:92-97`
- Bug: at any non-zero amount the full body+noise+click+modeInject sum is run through `recipSqrt` even when the AM term ≈0; recipSqrt(0.7)=0.573 (−18%). Discontinuous compressor inserted by a "character" knob.
- Fix: saturate only the AM-added component, or only when the signal exceeds threshold; keep the amount=0 exact-bypass behavior across the whole range.

**M-4. NonlinearCoupling AM is near-inaudible (transient-only, vanishes in sustain)** — `nonlinear_coupling.h:75-92`
- Bug: modulation `= 1 + vel·amt·8·dEnv`; `dEnv→0` in steady state so the effect is a brief attack-edge wiggle, not the promised "time-varying spectral centroid." Net effect of raising the knob is often a slight level cut (via M-3) rather than brightening.
- Fix: drive a real time-varying parameter (e.g. modulate a filter cutoff or per-mode brightness) rather than envelope-delta AM.

**M-5. decaySkew degrades to ±30% global decay nudge for Plate/Shell/Bell/NoiseBody** — `plate_mapper.h:69; shell_mapper.h:69; bell_mapper.h:64; noise_body_mapper.h:109`
- Bug: only Membrane applies the per-mode amplitude tilt `ratio^(−decaySkew)`; the other four bodies get only a `[0.7,1.3]` scalar on global decay (near JND, no spectral tilt).
- Fix: add the per-mode tilt term to the other four mappers.

**M-6. Secondary shell bank dropped entirely in `processBlockSlow` (FeedbackExciter path)** — `drum_voice.h:813-890` (present 691-734)
- Bug: `useSlowPath = feedbackExciter` is independent of coupling config; the slow path has no `secondaryBank_`/`effectiveCoupling_`. Feedback-exciter + coupling pads silently lose all shell character; two documented params become no-ops. (Not in factory presets → medium.)
- Fix: replicate the Phase-8D shell stage in the slow path (or block-process the shell after the per-sample feedback loop).

**M-7. NoiseLayer x3 gain calibrated for Bandpass but voice forces Lowpass** — `noise_layer.h:210-218` + `drum_voice.h:112`
- Bug: the 3× constant assumes a normalized 0 dB-peak bandpass (~0.1 RMS); a lowpass passes far more energy, so the parallel noise sits hotter than its "comparable to the modal body" target (worst at high cutoff). Feeds H-2.
- Fix: re-calibrate the standalone gain against the actual lowpass response, or apply per-mode energy normalization.

**M-8. Fast same-pad retrigger / re-steal hard-cuts when shadow slot is busy (click)** — `voice_pool.cpp:925-1001,196-248`
- Bug: `beginFastRelease()` is idempotent; on a same-slot Steal+NoteOn within the ~5 ms fade the swap is skipped and the ringing main voice is overwritten with a fresh attack → discontinuity. Single-shadow-per-slot reservation degrades to a hard cut. Narrow timing window (>~200 Hz same-pad), untested. Not a sameness contributor.
- Fix: when the shadow is occupied, zero/ramp the main voice before re-noteOn, or extend/queue the fade.

**M-9. Voice/pool path is mono — no per-pad pan or decorrelation** — `voice_pool.cpp:350-356,451-457`
- Bug: `outL[i]+=scratch; outR[i]+=scratch` for every voice; `scratchR_` allocated but unused; `PadConfig` has no pan field; master sums to mono. All 32 pads dead-center. Secondary contributor to a flat/lifeless image (doesn't homogenize timbre per se).
- Fix: add a per-pad pan field + equal-power pan law in the pool (and a future stereo body for decorrelation).

## 3. SCIENTIFIC-CORRECTNESS FINDINGS

### A) The math is RIGHT, and wired up right (NOT sameness causes)

- **Membrane Bessel ratios + air loading** — CONFIRMED, strong. `kMembraneRatios` match A&S to 4 decimals; airLoading morphs (m,1) modes exactly onto Rossing's measured timpani series `1:1.5:2:2.44:2.9`. Sweeping airLoading is one of the few stages that *should* produce a clear timbral change. Minor nuance: cos(mθ) angular factor dropped (slight m≥1 over-weighting); only 5/48 modes shifted (faithful to data limits). (Wikipedia circular-membrane table; Euphonics 3.6; Fletcher & Rossing.)
- **Chaigne damping law `R = b1 + b3·f²`** — CONFIRMED, strong. Exactly Chaigne & Askenfelt (1994) string-loss form; per-mode HF damping works; t60 ranges physically plausible (low-mode 0.14–35 s). Nuances (non-bugs): omits the Chaigne-Lambourg *linear* `c2·f` plate/membrane term; `b3` ceiling is a perceptual control, not a fitted constant. **The damping faithfully carries decay/material differences — whether they survive depends on the output clip (H-1), not this law.** (Chaigne & Askenfelt 1994; Chaigne & Lambourg 2001.)
- **Membrane strike weighting `|J_m(j_mn·r/a)|`** — CONFIRMED for the default body. Correct radial mode shape; Strike Position works on Membrane. (Wikipedia; JASA 150:202; Bilbao.)
- **Shell free-free Euler-Bernoulli *ratios*** — CONFIRMED numerically (`1, 2.757, 5.404, 8.933, 13.344, 18.638`). (Euphonics 3.2.1; F&R Ch.2.)
- **Bell tuned-partial *ratios* (first 7)** — CONFIRMED: normalized-to-nominal `0.25,0.50,0.60,0.75,1.00,1.50,2.00` = canonical hum/prime/tierce/quint/nominal/twelfth/double-octave. (Strike-tone/Rossing; Hibberts.)
- **Friction (bow) exciter** — CONFIRMED verbatim STK BowTable `clamp((|Δv·slope+offset|+0.75)^−4)`, slope `5−4·pressure`. (STK BowTable; Smith PASP.)
- **WaveguideString loss filter + pick comb** — CONFIRMED. Jaffe-Smith one-zero loop filter `g·[(1−S)+S·z⁻¹]` with S∈[0,0.5] (monotonic HF darkening, min-phase) and pick comb `1−z⁻ᴹ`. String body genuinely responds to Material/StrikePos. (Jaffe & Smith 1983; Karplus & Strong 1983.)
- **Tension-modulation pitch glide** — CONFIRMED in mechanism/direction/`velocity²` scaling (Avanzini-Marogna-Bank 2012: ΔT_avg ∝ E). Nuances: linear `1+amt·E` instead of `√(1+kE)` (fine within ≤2-semitone budget); energy follower reads post-softClip output, not modal energy. **Real wiring bug:** tension code is absent from `processBlockSlow` (FeedbackExciter gets no glide). And it's off-by-default — exposing `tensionModAmt_` in presets would recover an expressive axis.

### B) The math is WRONG or OVERSIMPLIFIED (contributes to sameness)

- **Plate/cymbal uses SSSS `(m²+n²)/2` ratios** — OVERSIMPLIFIED, strong. `plate_modes.h:42-111`. A real cymbal/gong (free edges, often non-flat) follows a Chladni power law in `(m+2n)`: classical `(m+2n)²`, modified-measured `C_n·(m+2n)^P_n` with empirical exponents ~1.73/1.60 (Rossing). SSSS describes a hinged/pinned plate — a struck cymbal is not pinned. The coded table is quasi-regular with exact degenerate repeats (12.5, 25.0 appear twice), *more* ordered than a real inharmonic cymbal. Since the ratio topology is fixed, Size/Material/modeStretch/modeScatter only scale/tilt the same skeleton → metallic presets converge. Also missing the cymbal/gong dynamic nonlinearity (pitch glide, HF energy cascade). **Correct form:** free-plate eigenvalues (Leissa free-free / Waller) or `(m+2n)^P` Chladni fit. (F&R; Rossing & Peterson JASA; Chladni/Kirchhoff; Waller 1939; Fletcher 1999.) → *Must land before re-tuning metallic kits.*
- **Stretch inharmonicity: form right, index wrong, range tiny** — NUANCED, strong. `modal_resonator_bank.h:670,687`. `√(1+B·k²)` is correct stiff-string form, but uses 0-based array index `k` → computes `√(1+B·k²)` instead of `√(1+B·(k+1)²)`: fundamental left unstretched, every partial gets the dispersion of partial `m−1` (the bow-weight code at :608 correctly uses `k+1`, confirming the anomaly). And `B_max=0.001` is orders of magnitude below real piano/bar values. So the Stretch knob barely moves partials and mistunes them. **Fix:** index `(k+1)` and widen `B_max`. (Russell PSU; F&R; Young 1952; arXiv:1603.05516.)
- **`mode_inject` uses `1/k²` mislabeled "natural harmonic falloff"** — OVERSIMPLIFIED, strong. `mode_inject.h:46-55`. `1/k²` (−12 dB/oct) is the *triangle*/plucked-envelope spectrum, not the generic natural (sawtooth/bowed) `1/k` (−6 dB/oct). 8th partial is 36 dB down vs 18 dB for `1/k`, so the injected series is fundamental-dominated and dull; the ModeInject knob adds dark low-harmonic tone rather than distinctive timbre. **Fix:** default to `1/k` or a tunable exponent. (F&R; ThinkDSP; Triangle/Sawtooth-wave refs.)
- **Shell strike shape is simply-supported `sin(k·π·x)`, not free-free** — OVERSIMPLIFIED, strong. `shell_modes.h:48-57`. Ratios are free-free but the mode shape is pinned: at the free ends (x=0, x=L — the most common strike region) the true free-free shape is a *maximum* antinode (|φ|=1) while `sin` is identically *zero*. With the abs()+0.05 floor, an edge strike collapses the whole mode set to a flat floor → same dull spectrum regardless of params. And the secondary shell hard-codes `strikePos=0.3`, so coupling timbre never responds to Strike Position. **Fix:** use the real free-free eigenfunction (small per-mode `βₙL`/`σₙ` lookup) or at least a `|cos(k·π·x/L)|` family with end antinodes. (F&R Ch.2; Rossing & Fletcher §2.19.)
- **Bell strike shape `|cos((k+1)·π·r)|` is not a bell mode shape** — OVERSIMPLIFIED, strong. `bell_modes.h:56-65`. Bell modes are 2-D `(m nodal meridians, n nodal circles)`; partial ordering is decoupled from any single radial index, so binding amplitude to `(k+1)` radial half-waves has no physical correspondence to the partial it multiplies. Plus a real clapper strikes a *fixed* soundbow (`r≈R`), so sweeping r is unphysical; the 0.05 floor blunts what's left. Strike Position has weak/non-characteristic authority on bells. **Fix:** meridional `cos(mθ)` × through-thickness `φₙ` at the strike location. (Rossing Science of Percussion Ch.5; Perrin et al. 1983/1985; Hibberts 2014.)

### C) The math is right but WIRED UP WRONG / dropped (the most actionable physics items)

- **`1/sqrt(N)` energy norm + dead amplitude-aware compensation** — OVERSIMPLIFIED, strong. `1/sqrt(N)` is the correct norm only for N *uncorrelated* steady-state contributions. A struck body launches all modes *in phase* → the attack peak is the *coherent* sum `~Σ|aᵢ|`, not `√N`. Faust prescribes `1/N`, Smith/CCRMA uses per-mode fitted gains with *no* mode-count divisor; the code misattributes `1/sqrt(N)` to Faust (which uses `1/N`). The intended `Σ(amp)·0.707` make-up that would restore amplitude-awareness is **dead code** (the bank's own `getInputGainSum()` is unused). Net: per-preset loudness/amplitude differences are normalized away, and the under-divided coherent attack runs hotter into H-1's clip. **Fix:** use `getInputGainSum()` (`1/Σ|aᵢ|` unit-peak) or Faust's `1/N`, and verify the attack isn't silently clipped. (Smith PASP Modal Expansion; Faust physmodels.lib; F&R.)
- **Head↔shell coupling is feedforward-only, not bidirectional** — OVERSIMPLIFIED, strong. Real coupling is bidirectional and produces **mode-frequency splitting** + **beating** (slow energy exchange) — both intrinsic to two-way coupling and *impossible* with feedforward injection. The stability fix (drop the return path) over-corrected: literature (Bank & Karjalainen 2010; Bilbao 2009) keeps a *passive reciprocal* coupling stable without deleting feedback — the "continuous beep" was an *active/non-passive* coupling, not proof bidirectional is unstable. So changing secondarySize/Material/coupling only adds a static ringing layer rather than re-shaping the body's modes. Off-by-default limits real-world blame; where enabled, it under-differentiates. **Fix:** passive reciprocal coupling with bounded coupling energy. (F&R two-mass model; Bank & Karjalainen DAFx 2010; Bilbao NSS 2009.)
- **Per-voice tanh contradicts STK/Faust/Bilbao** — this is the scientific framing of H-1: none of the canonical modal frameworks put a tanh on the bank/voice output; saturation, when present, is an explicit gain-staged drive. The always-on curve at the hottest node is the dynamics/timbre flattener.

## 4. SAMENESS DIAGNOSIS — stages ordered by how much fixing restores variety

1. **Per-voice softClip as gain stage (H-1/H-4)** — flattens peak dynamics (vel 0.5 == vel 1.0), pins loud pads to one ceiling, imposes one tanh signature. *Biggest single win.*
2. **Body buried under noise/click + dead body make-up (H-2 / §C `1/sqrt(N)`)** — the only timbre-carrying stage is ~17 dB down; the ear hears a generic noisy thwack. *Unmasks Material/Size/StrikePos/airLoading/damping.*
3. **Pitch envelope + Material Morph dead on 5/6 bodies (H-3/M-1)** — removes the most salient motion axis (pitch drop) from every non-Membrane preset.
4. **Plate/cymbal SSSS ratios + weak Stretch + `1/k²` ModeInject + shell/bell strike shapes (§B)** — the inharmonic fingerprints that separate metallic timbres aren't in the parameter space; ModeInject and Stretch barely move things.
5. **Drive compressor / NonlinearCoupling recipSqrt (M-2/M-3/M-4)** — presets with these engaged converge toward the same saturated knee; the coupling knob barely registers.
6. **decaySkew scalar-only on 4 bodies (M-5)** — decay-reshape knob near-inert off Membrane.
7. **Mono image (M-9)** — shared flat centered picture; secondary "lifeless" contributor, not a timbral homogenizer.

Parameters with weak/dead audible effect: **pitch-env Start/End/Time/Curve/Knee/Mid** (5/6 bodies), **Material Morph** (5/6 bodies), **per-pad Level** (swamped by clip), **Coupling/Secondary** (off-by-default + feedforward + dropped in slow path), **ModeInject amount** (`1/k²` too dark), **Stretch** (off-by-one + tiny B), **decaySkew** (4 bodies), **NonlinearCoupling** (transient-only AM), **Strike Position on Plate/Bell/Shell** (clamped / wrong shape / hard-coded).

## 5. SUGGESTED FIX ORDER (smallest change → highest impact; gain-staging gates re-tuning)

**Phase 0 — gain staging (MUST precede any preset re-tuning):**
1. **H-1 + H-4:** make the per-voice output linear; move Level and master makeup *after* a downstream true-peak/brickwall limiter (or the existing master energy limiter). Single-site change at `drum_voice.h:446/769/782/885`, the highest-impact fix.
2. **§C `1/sqrt(N)` + L-1:** replace the body norm with `1/getInputGainSum()` (unit-peak) and delete the dead `bodyGainCompensation_`/`secondaryGainCompensation_` branches + stale comments.
3. **H-2 + M-7:** re-balance noise(×3)/click(×2) standalone gains against the corrected body level (and the actual Lowpass response). After 1–3, add a regression test asserting per-velocity peak scaling (catches H-1) and a coupling-on-vs-off energy test (closes L-9).

**Phase 1 — restore dead modulation axes (still pre-tuning for affected bodies):**
4. **H-3 + M-1:** extend `updateBodyFundamental()`/morph refresh + mapper `pitchHz` consumption to all body types.
5. **M-6 + tension slow-path:** add the secondary shell stage AND tension/pitch glide to `processBlockSlow`; fix the stale stability comments (L-8) and raise the vestigial 0.25 ceiling (L-7).

**Phase 2 — physics corrections (MUST precede re-tuning of metallic / inharmonic kits):**
6. **Stretch index `(k+1)` + widen `B_max`** (small, high-value), **`mode_inject` → `1/k`** (one-line default), **decaySkew per-mode tilt on 4 bodies (M-5)**.
