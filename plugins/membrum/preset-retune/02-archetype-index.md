# Membrum drum-archetype recipe library (Stage A)

85 archetypes researched. Each recipe maps the real drum's acoustics (cited web research) to exact normalized Membrum parameter values, against the corrected post-audit DSP semantics. The per-archetype files carry the full exact-value param tables + rationale + sources; `recipes.json` is the machine-readable map; `00-param-dictionary.md`/`param-dictionary.json` define every Membrum parameter; `01-current-state.md`/`current-state.json` inventory the 20 current factory kits.

- [808 Tom (boom-glide) — tom](archetypes/808-tom-boom-glide-tom.md) — Membrane (offset 1, norm 0.0). Membrane f0 = 500·0.1^size. The 808 tom is NOT a struck acoustic membrane — it is a self-oscillating bridged-T SINE with a downward pitch sweep. We pick Membrane (not String/Plate) because: (a) the pitch glide is driven by the ToneShaper pitch envelope, which the audit H-3 fix made work on ALL bodies, so any modal body would glide — but Membrane's low-order Bessel ratios collapse to a near-pure fundamental when material is low and b3 damping kills the overtones (audit §3-A: Membrane Bessel + Chaigne damping CONFIRMED correct), giving the tuned-sine 'boom'; (b) all the membrane-specific PHYSICS axes (airLoading, tension-mod) are deliberately disabled so the body behaves as a tuned resonator, not an acoustic drumhead. This matches the existing 808ElectronicKit tom builder (Mallet+Membrane), tools/membrum_preset_generator.cpp:834-863. body / Mallet (offset 0, norm 0.2 → idx round(0.2·5)=1=Mallet). A soft beater deposits energy gently and band-limited (little HF), so the excited body rings as a near-sine 'tonk' rather than a bright struck-skin crack — the closest match to the 808's self-oscillating sine that rings briefly and fades (Baratatronix: 'self-dampening sine wave that rings briefly when triggered'). Impulse (raised-cosine click) would add too much broadband attack energy; Mallet is the documented choice in the existing 808 tom builder. exciter

- [Tambourine (jingles) — perc](archetypes/tambourine-jingles-perc.md) — NoiseBody (offset 1 = 5). NoiseBody = a plate-ratio modal bank (32 inharmonic metallic modes) PLUS an internal filtered-noise layer — the correct model for a no-definite-pitch, inharmonic, broadband-metallic idiophone whose strike spectrum "moves upward through the spectrum and rings at a few kHz" (SOS metallic-percussion analysis). The plate-ratio bank gives the inharmonic skeleton of the zils; the internal noise layer + the always-on parallel noise layer give the dense, noise-like shimmer of many small discs colliding. A secondary Shell bank (secondaryEnabled) is added to capture the discrete inharmonic metallic jingle ring on top of the broadband body. body / NoiseBurst (offset 0 = 2). The NoiseBurstExciter fires a short violet-noise bandpass burst into the body — physically this is the broadband "many-discs-rattling-at-once" contact event, not a single clean impulse. It seeds the metallic modes with HF-weighted energy (violet = +6 dB/oct), matching the bright, HF-dominated attack of a jingle hit. Burst duration kept short (≈6 ms) so the attack reads as a crisp shake/tap rather than a sustained scrape. exciter

- [Trap Sub 808 Kick (kick)](archetypes/trap-sub-808-kick-kick.md) — Membrane (BodyModelType::Membrane, norm 0.0). A struck circular drumhead Bessel bank is the closest physical model to the TR-808's bridged-T self-oscillating sine: at large Size the low Bessel modes are spread far apart and the b3·f² damping is near-zero on the fundamental, so the body decays as an essentially pure low sine — the 808's signature. Critically, Membrane is the ONLY body the tension-modulation pitch glide (offset 58) is gated to, and an exaggerated tension glide is core to this archetype, so the body choice is forced. body / Impulse (ExciterType::Impulse, norm 0.0). A raised-cosine click is the digital analogue of the 808's ~1 ms trigger pulse that 'kicks' the bridged-T oscillator into motion. It deposits energy into the modes in-phase and gets out of the way, leaving the body to ring as a decaying sine. Mallet would soften the contact (less 808-like snap); NoiseBurst/Friction/Feedback all add sustained or broadband character that fights the clean sub. The audible front-of-transient 'tick' is supplied by the dedicated Click layer instead, which is the right tool (tunable contact time + brightness, half-fed into the body and half to output). exciter

- [Tubular Bell / Chimes (tuned, long-ring metallic tube)](archetypes/tubular-bell-chimes-tuned-long-ring-metallic-tube.md) — String (Krate::DSP::WaveguideString — Jaffe-Smith digital waveguide, NOT a modal bank). Chosen because a long hanging metal tube struck at the top cap behaves as a single dominant sustained resonator with a long, even-decaying metallic tail; the waveguide's lossy loop filter + pick-comb gives exactly that "one pitched ring that sustains for seconds" character without the dense modal-bank attack. The true free-free-bar inharmonic partial ladder (1 : 2.76 : 5.40 : 8.93…) is approximated, not reproduced literally — the String body trades the literal P4/P5/P6 strike-tone triplet for a sustaining single-pitch tube ring, which is the perceptually dominant feature of orchestral chimes. body / Mallet (ExciterType=1). A rawhide/plastic-headed mallet striking the top edge is a soft, short beater contact — the Mallet exciter's softer raised-cosine beater (vs the hard Impulse click) matches the real excitation and avoids over-exciting the highest waveguide harmonics. The crisp metal "tink" of the cap contact is supplied separately by the always-on Click layer rather than by a hard exciter. exciter
