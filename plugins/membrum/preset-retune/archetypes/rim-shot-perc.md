## Membrum Recipe: Rim Shot (perc)

Loud bright snare-layer cracking accent, brighter and louder than the side-stick.

**Body:** Shell free-free bar (norm 0.4), inharmonic partials 1:2.757:5.404 — the metallic short-ring resonator, analogue of the TR-909's three detuned twin-T resonators (~220/500/1000 Hz, pulse-fired, heavily damped; network-909).
**Exciter:** Impulse (norm 0.0), the hardest cleanest transient.

**Voicing:** f0 ~686 Hz (Size 0.34) puts the Shell partials in the 3-6 kHz rim-crack band (head-fi/tapetown); the low head-thump is left to the Click+Noise layers per the corrected gain-staging. Maximal Click (mix 0.94 / contact ~2.6 ms / brightness ~9.4 kHz) is the wood-on-metal crack and half-feeds the exciter (b1/b3 click partials). Dead body damping (b1=25 s^-1, b3=3e-4) keeps the metallic ping short — crack, not bell. modeScatter 0.4 + modeStretch 0.45 add the jagged inharmonicity real rim metal has (USPTO 7473840). Thin violet noise (0.2, ~7.4 kHz, ~32 ms) supplies sizzle. macroPunch 0.92 near max; Level 0.9 (louder than side-stick 0.78). No pitch glide, no morph/drive/fold/mode-inject/coupling/tension. Pan center.
